--- title: "Argument Role Labelling Question-Answer Pair Data Augmentation" excerpt: "" collection: portfolio --- One sentence summary: Augment question-answer pairs with semantic and syntactic constraints to solve the argument role labeling task. This work explores the QA augmentation for events in news articles. Our altimate goal is to automatically extract structured events from unlabeled unstructured articles. In this work, we focus on a subtask: extract QA pairs for arguments given an event. The synthetic data severs as augmented data to train the QA event extraction model.

The core theory of this approach is tracking the dynamics of entities and their attributes, such as 'avatar 1' with attribute 'at location 1' or 'at location 2', in a series of unit actions. We decompose a complicated goal into a bunch of reusable unit actions, e.g. 'go', 'get'. The unit action is viewed as an external method to alter the internal states of the entity. Our focus is on the dynamics of these entity states. 
init: (avatar_1 at location_1)
go: (avatar_1 at location_2) # avatar_1's location attribute changes from location_1 to location_2
get: (avatar_1 has item_1) # avatar_1's inventory attribute adds item_1
goal: (avatar_1 has item_1)
