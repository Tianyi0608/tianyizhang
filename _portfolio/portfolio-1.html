--- title: "PROC2PDDL: Predicting Domain Definitions Based on Natural Language for Symbolic Planning" excerpt: "" collection: portfolio ---

One sentence summary: Reasoning on the changes of entities and states when performing an action in symbolic language.

This work explores the causal reasoning of events. It treats causal links as the pre- and post-conditions between actions, represented in the symbolic language. To implement a sequence of actions, the pre-conditions should be satisfied before executing each action, and the post-conditions will be acquired after the action. We generate the pre- and post-conditions of actions. Then, a PDDL solver finds a path to achieve a goal.

# An action go requires (as pre-condition) an avatar 1 at location 1 and location 1 can be connected to location 2; 
# the result is (as post-condition) an avatar 1 at location 2.

(: action go
	parameters: avatar_1, location_1, location_2
	pre-conditions: (avatar_1 at location_1) (location_1 connect to location_2)
	post-conditions: (avatar_1 at location_2)
)

The core theory of this approach is tracking the dynamics of entities and their attributes, such as 'avatar 1' with attribute 'at location 1', in a series of unit actions. It decomposes the accomplishment of a complicated goal into the fulfilment of a series of reusable unit actions (with particular conditions), e.g. 'go'.

init: (avatar_1 at location_1)
go: (avatar_1 at location_2) # avatar_1's location attribute changes from location_1 to location_2
get: (avatar_1 has item_1) # avatar_1's inventory attribute adds item_1
goal: (avatar_1 has item_1)
